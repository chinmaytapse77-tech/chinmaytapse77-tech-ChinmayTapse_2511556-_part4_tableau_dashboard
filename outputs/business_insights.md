# Business Insights — Executive Dashboard

This document presents 9 data-driven insights derived from the executive dashboard. Each insight follows a consistent structure: **Observation → Evidence → Interpretation → Recommended Action**.

All figures cover the Jan 2024 – Dec 2025 period (4,200 orders, 4,096 unique customers, $217.0M total sales).

---

## Insight 1 — Sales Trend: Modest YoY growth with a clear August seasonal peak

**Observation:** Monthly sales fluctuate between $6.3M and $10.9M with no strong directional trend, but August 2025 set the record high.

**Evidence:**
- 2024 total sales: $106.24M
- 2025 total sales: $110.78M (**+4.3% YoY**)
- Peak month: **August 2025 ($10.86M)**
- Lowest month: August 2024 ($6.30M) — note the dramatic August recovery YoY (+72%)
- Months above $10M: 8 out of 24

**Interpretation:** Growth is modest (4.3% is below most retail benchmarks), but the August 2025 spike suggests a successful promotion or seasonal initiative that didn't exist in August 2024. The pattern is not driven by linear growth — it's lumpy with periodic peaks.

**Recommended action:** Identify what changed in August 2025 (campaigns, inventory, pricing) and assess whether it's reproducible. If it was a planned initiative, scale it; if a one-off external event, set realistic expectations for 2026. Investigate why August 2024 was the trough month — operational issue, stockout, or campaign gap?

## Insight 2 — Regional Performance: South leads in absolute sales, East has highest margin

**Observation:** South is the largest region by sales, but East quietly delivers the highest profitability per dollar of sales.

**Evidence:**

| Region | Sales | Profit | Margin | Return Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| South | $64.7M | $9.99M | 15.4% | 5.0% |
| North | $54.6M | $8.31M | 15.2% | 4.0% |
| West | $48.9M | $7.40M | 15.1% | 4.0% |
| East | $48.9M | $7.60M | **15.6%** | 5.0% |

**Interpretation:** Sales and profit are remarkably even across regions (margin range only 0.5pp). South wins on volume; East wins on efficiency. North and East have similar sales but East delivers $0.5M more profit on a smaller customer base. The 5% return rate in South and East deserves attention — slightly above the 4.5% company average.

**Recommended action:** South is the priority for growth investments. East is the lab to learn from — its margin advantage may come from product mix, pricing discipline, or smaller average discounts. Investigate East's discount distribution. For South and East, dig into what drives the elevated return rate before investing in either further.

## Insight 3 — Category Profitability: Technology generates 84% of profit on 71% of sales

**Observation:** Technology is the engine of profitability; Furniture is a drag on margin despite being the second-largest sales category.

**Evidence:**

| Category | Sales | % Sales | Profit | % Profit | Margin | Return Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Technology | $153.9M | 70.9% | $28.04M | 84.2% | **18.2%** | 3.0% |
| Furniture | $51.6M | 23.8% | $3.56M | 10.7% | **6.9%** | 7.7% |
| Office Supplies | $11.5M | 5.3% | $1.71M | 5.1% | 14.9% | 3.7% |

**Interpretation:** Technology punches well above its weight on profit and has the lowest return rate. Furniture sells second-best by volume but produces a meager 6.9% margin AND has the highest return rate (7.7% — 2.5× the company average). This is the single biggest profitability problem in the business.

**Recommended action:** Audit the Furniture category urgently. Are returns driven by damaged-in-transit issues, sizing mismatches, or product quality? A 7.7% return rate destroys the already-thin Furniture margins. Consider re-pricing, simplifying the SKU range, or improving packaging. Meanwhile, look for opportunities to grow Technology share of mix (currently 70.9% sales → make it 75%+).

## Insight 4 — Sub-Category: Four products carry the business

**Observation:** Four sub-categories account for ~84% of total profit. The bottom nine produce a long tail of small contributions.

**Evidence:** Top profit contributors:

| Sub-Category | Sales | Profit | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Copiers | $40.6M | $7.31M | 18.0% |
| Accessories | $39.2M | $7.19M | 18.3% |
| Phones | $38.4M | $7.10M | 18.5% |
| Machines | $35.7M | $6.45M | 18.1% |
| **Top 4 total** | **$153.9M** | **$28.05M** | **18.2%** |

Bottom 5 sub-categories (Paper, Binders, Storage, Art, Labels) collectively contribute $1.7M profit on $11.5M sales (14.8% margin) — useful for completing the catalog but not strategic levers.

**Interpretation:** The "top 4" are all Technology products. Their performance is what's driving the strong Technology category. They are remarkably uniform (margins 18.0%–18.5%), suggesting a consistent pricing/cost structure across the high-value tech catalog.

**Recommended action:** Treat Copiers, Accessories, Phones, and Machines as a strategic group. Inventory availability for these four sub-categories should never dip; promotional priority should default to them. The bottom 5 sub-categories are candidates for SKU rationalization — fewer SKUs, faster turnover.

## Insight 5 — Customer Segment: Home Office is the largest but has highest return risk

**Observation:** All three customer segments perform similarly on sales, but Home Office has a meaningfully higher return rate.

**Evidence:**

| Segment | Sales | Profit | Margin | Orders | Return Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Home Office | $74.5M | $11.56M | 15.5% | 1,446 | **5.67%** |
| Consumer | $71.9M | $11.03M | 15.3% | 1,355 | 3.91% |
| Corporate | $70.6M | $10.72M | 15.2% | 1,399 | 4.00% |

**Interpretation:** Home Office is the largest segment by both sales and profit, but its return rate is ~45% higher than the other two segments. This may reflect that small-office buyers have less rigid procurement processes and are more likely to return — or that the product mix sold to Home Office (perhaps more Furniture) drives returns up.

**Recommended action:** Cross-tabulate Home Office returns by category — confirm whether the elevated return rate is concentrated in Furniture. If yes, then this is the same Furniture issue from Insight 3 wearing a different hat. If no, investigate whether Home Office customers receive different post-purchase communication, fitment guidance, or warranty handling.

## Insight 6 — Discount Impact: 20% is the break-even cliff

**Observation:** Profit margin collapses sharply once discount exceeds 20%, and turns negative above 30%.

**Evidence:**

| Discount band | Orders | Total Profit | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-5% | 1,529 | $19.17M | **19.8%** |
| 5-10% | 474 | $4.15M | 15.8% |
| 10-15% | 511 | $3.18M | 14.1% |
| 15-20% | 536 | $3.46M | 13.2% |
| 20-25% | 558 | $1.74M | **8.6%** |
| 25-30% | 528 | $1.72M | **7.6%** |
| 30%+ | 64 | **−$0.10M** | **−5.0%** |

**Interpretation:** This is the cleanest, most actionable finding in the data. Margins are robust at discounts below 20%, but collapse roughly in half once discount exceeds 20%, and become loss-making above 30%. Yet 558+528+64 = **1,150 orders** (27% of all orders) were sold at >20% discount.

**Recommended action:** Impose a soft cap on discounts at 20% unless a specific business justification exists. The 64 orders above 30% lost money in aggregate. Even orders at 25-30% are generating only $3,254 average profit each vs $12,536 at 0-5%. Reviewing the discount approval process is the highest-ROI lever in this dataset.

## Insight 7 — Shipping Performance: Standard Class dominates but Same Day has best customer outcomes

**Observation:** 58% of orders ship Standard Class (the slowest non-trivial option), yet Same Day shipping shows materially better return rates.

**Evidence:**

| Ship Mode | Orders | % of Total | Avg Delivery (days) | Return Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard Class | 2,435 | 58% | 4.71 | 5% |
| Second Class | 894 | 21% | 2.68 | 5% |
| First Class | 630 | 15% | 1.77 | 5% |
| **Same Day** | **241** | **6%** | **0.40** | **2%** |

By delivery bucket:

| Delivery Bucket | Orders | Return Rate | Avg Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fast (0-2 days) | 1,203 | 4.4% | 4.06 |
| Standard (3-4 days) | 1,570 | 4.9% | 4.09 |
| Slow (5-6 days) | 1,224 | 4.2% | 4.07 |
| **Very Slow (7+ days)** | 203 | 4.4% | **3.89** |

**Interpretation:** Same Day shipping has half the return rate of any other mode. Customer ratings drop noticeably (from ~4.07 to 3.89) only when delivery extends to 7+ days. Standard Class is the workhorse, but the data does not show that faster shipping reduces returns — except for Same Day, which is meaningfully different.

**Recommended action:** Investigate what makes Same Day different. Is it the product mix (small high-value items), the customer profile (less likely to change their mind), or the operational process (more careful handling)? If any of these can be ported to Standard Class, expect a return-rate improvement. Also: identify which orders ended up in the 7+ day bucket and prevent recurrence — they're a customer-experience risk.

## Insight 8 — Return Patterns: Concentrated in Furniture, especially in Home Office segment

**Observation:** Returns are not evenly distributed — they cluster in a specific category-segment intersection.

**Evidence:**
- Overall return rate: 4.55% (191 of 4,200 orders)
- Furniture return rate: **7.67%** (vs Technology 3.03%, Office Supplies 3.65%)
- Home Office return rate: 5.67% (vs Consumer 3.91%, Corporate 4.00%)
- Returns also slightly elevated in East (5%) and South (5%) regions

**Interpretation:** The story converges: Furniture sold to Home Office customers, particularly in East and South regions, is the highest-risk slice of the business. This compounds the margin pressure from Insight 3 — Furniture not only has thin margins, it also has the highest return rate, which further erodes net profitability.

**Recommended action:** Triple-click on Furniture → Home Office returns. Specific actions to consider:
1. Are returns concentrated in specific Furniture sub-categories (Tables vs Furnishings vs Bookcases)?
2. Is there a damaged-in-transit pattern by ship mode?
3. Would a 30-day satisfaction-guarantee policy reduce or worsen the rate?
4. Could product photos / sizing information be improved for Home Office buyers?

## Insight 9 — Campaign Channel: Organic dominates, but Referral has highest order value

**Observation:** Organic search drives the most orders, but Referral customers spend more per order on average.

**Evidence:**

| Channel | Sales | Orders | Avg Order Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organic | $88.8M | 1,694 | $52,411 |
| Paid | $40.1M | 849 | $47,245 |
| Email | $34.5M | 657 | $52,530 |
| Social | $31.1M | 584 | $53,261 |
| **Referral** | $21.2M | 392 | **$54,111** |

**Interpretation:** Organic is the volume driver — 40% of orders come through it, and it requires no per-order cost. Referral has the highest average order value (+3% vs Organic), suggesting word-of-mouth customers are higher-quality. Paid channels show the lowest AOV ($47K), implying the paid funnel may be capturing more bargain-hunters or smaller buyers.

**Recommended action:** Invest in referral programs — they bring in higher-value customers with no per-acquisition cost beyond the reward. Audit Paid channel spend efficiency; if Paid AOV is materially below other channels even after marketing cost is accounted for, the spend may not be earning its keep. Confirm Organic stays well-supported (SEO, organic content) — it is the largest revenue source and the cheapest channel by ad spend.

## Calculated Fields Used in the Dashboard

The following calculated fields underpin the analysis:

| Field | Formula | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `Profit Margin` | `SUM([profit]) / SUM([sales])` | Margin % at any aggregation |
| `Cost` | `[sales] - [profit]` | Implied cost of goods sold |
| `Average Order Value` | `SUM([sales]) / COUNTD([order_id])` | Per-order revenue |
| `Return Rate` | `SUM([return_flag]) / COUNTD([order_id])` | Returns as % of orders |
| `Shipping Delay Bucket` | `IF [delivery_days] <= 2 THEN "Fast (0-2 days)" ELSEIF [delivery_days] <= 4 THEN "Standard (3-4 days)" ELSEIF [delivery_days] <= 6 THEN "Slow (5-6 days)" ELSE "Very Slow (7+ days)" END` | Group delivery times for analysis |
| `Profit per Order` | `SUM([profit]) / COUNTD([order_id])` | Per-order profitability |
| `YoY Sales` | `SUM(IF YEAR([order_date]) = 2025 THEN [sales] END) / SUM(IF YEAR([order_date]) = 2024 THEN [sales] END) - 1` | Year-over-year growth flag |
| `High Discount Flag` | `IF [discount] > 0.20 THEN "Risk Zone" ELSE "Safe Zone" END` | Surface the 20% discount cliff |

These are exposed as Tableau calculated fields and reused across multiple views in the workbook.
